Legal Procedures for Resolving Common Disputes

Below are the steps you can follow to resolve various legal issues without the help of an advocate.

Property Dispute Resolution Procedure

  1. Identify the ownership records of the property in question.
  2. Try mediation with the other party for an out-of-court settlement.
  3. File a complaint with the local revenue office if mediation fails.
  4. Approach the civil court if the issue persists after official complaints.

Example: In Tamil Nadu, if two neighbors dispute the boundary of their properties, they can first try mediation through local panchayat. If unresolved, they can file a complaint with the district revenue officer.

Consumer Dispute Resolution Procedure

  1. Contact the seller or service provider to request a replacement or refund.
  2. If unresolved, file a complaint with the Consumer Protection Forum.
  3. Provide all receipts and proof of purchase to support your case.
  4. The Consumer Forum will issue a verdict after reviewing the complaint.

Example: A customer in Tamil Nadu purchases a defective product online. After contacting the seller and failing to get a resolution, they file a case with the Tamil Nadu Consumer Court, and the court orders a refund.

Employment Dispute Resolution Procedure

  1. Request a formal meeting with the employer to resolve the issue internally.
  2. If unsuccessful, file a complaint with the labor office.
  3. The labor officer will mediate between you and the employer.
  4. If unresolved, you can take the matter to the Labor Court for legal proceedings.

Example: An employee in Chennai was wrongfully terminated. After approaching the labor office and attending mediation, the issue remains unresolved. The employee files a case in the Labor Court to get compensation.
